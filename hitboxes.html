Hitboxes

Hitboxes is an abstract exploration of the concept of the invisible geometry in video games that determine how things collide and—well—hit. In this project I wanted to demonstrate a simpler form of abstraction while also exploring a less fair form of hitbox. The subject is constricted to triangles that are obviously larger and less rectangular than the average hitbox. This is to showcase the obvious difference between video games—where hitboxes typically reside—and reality. Despite not making hard contact with the subject, there can still be external effects that occur near the subject that still affect the subject. To be completely frank, this is one of my least favorite and most uninspired projects, yet it still managed to make a personal connection to my own life. I suppose that’s just how art manifests itself.
