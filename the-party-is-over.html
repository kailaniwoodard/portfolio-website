Why Is It So Hard To Accept The Party Is Over

Why Is It So Hard To Accept The Party Is Over is a collection of partially staged images that hold their focus in imitating the atmosphere of college parties or perhaps the emotional processing associated with being under the influence in that sort of environment. This connects to the ultimate deeper meaning of escapism and mania associated with the artist’s very own disorders, those being bipolar disorder II and borderline personality disorder. Each of the images in this project incorporate purple lighting because of the color’s composition between red and blue. Red and blue are ultimately used as devices to convey the blend of depression and hypomania associated with bipolar disorder, with blue representing depression and red representing hypomania respectively. In the photos, the model is wearing a red top to represent the manic or hypomanic side of bipolar disorder and wears purple makeup to represent the mix between the two aspects of the disorder. The makeup also takes aesthetic inspiration from the American drama, Euphoria, to emphasize the association with struggles with substance abuse and the clear intent or lack of intent in “staying clean”. This allusion is used to further drive home the meaning behind Why Is It So Hard To Accept The Party Is Over. In a technical sense, the images are shot with a purple flash and edited to imitate the aesthetics of a disposable film camera. This is achieved through purple tape over the flash of the camera and editing within Adobe Photoshop to create the illusion of capturing “Kodak moments” of a young woman in distress in an environment that should foster anything but. The display is strategically planned to further emphasize the previously implied aesthetics through color by incorporating purple neon lighting and semi-transparent prints so that the light shines through the photos. Ultimately, the meaning of Why Is It So Hard To Accept The Party Is Over is the darker motivations behind social substance use masked by the ambiance of a party.
