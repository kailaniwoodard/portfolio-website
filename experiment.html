Performing Mad
Science in the Nick of Time...

Not really...

What's the Goal Here?

The goal of this experimentation, if you could really call what's going on here experimental in any form was to determine that even the most rudimentary form of my prototype for my proposed research would perform intended functions. The intended functions here are tracing eye movement using the prototype software that I developed.

How Are You Gonna Experiment That?

The primary issue that I came across in performing an experiment was finding what could be easily be experimented with the prototype I have developed.

The prototype that I developed was intended to demonstrate the ability to trace eye movement and show that I could add a visual indicator to go along with this traced movement to ensure that I could do so.

So What Did I Do?

I demonstrated to myself in an incredibly primitive way that I was able to collect data based on the position of the highlighted area in the video (this being the red circles that trace the movement of the pupil) and ensure by "eyeballing" it that it showed potential to produce results.

I admit that I struggled to develop a prototype for a while and that, among other things, contributed to my non-traditional approach to completing these preliminary steps to proposing my project. As a result, becoming happy with the product I created did not leave a lot of opportunity to experiment, however I feel that I created software or at least a good baseline for what technology needs created to pursue my intended research.
