Just Do It

Just Do It is a collection of partially staged images that holds focus in creating visual representations of the emotions behind manic episodes, specifically based on my own experiences. Each of the images in this project incorporate the color red because of the personal associations that I have with the color and also to represent the strong emotional turmoil associated with my personal manic episodes. In this scenario, the color red specifically calls out to the phrases “seeing red” and simultaneously “seeing the world through rose-colored glasses”. For me, when in a manic episode, it feels as if I am almost on top of the world; I feel a near unnecessary rush of happiness and energy and self-importance. To me, it feels as if I could almost do anything. These images go deeper than just hypothetical staged scenarios, they all represent specific instances that occurred during a manic episode. Three of the images use red trailing creatively to exaggerate and call attention to the focal points of each picture. In two of the images, it calls attention to my shoes because they are a personal connection to me, despite using a model to act out these scenarios. Also emblazoned on each of these images are the words “Just Do It” to represent the call of impulse associated with mania, which also pays homage to Nike because of my iconic Air Force 1 shoes that at the time, I was always wearing.
